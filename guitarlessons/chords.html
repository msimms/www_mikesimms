Chords

Definitions

A chord is defined as playing three or more notes simultaneously though in practice it often used when two more more tones are played at the same time. The technical term for playing two notes at once is a double stop.

Major Chords

A major chord is comprised of a root note along with the notes at it's 3rd and 5th intervals. A C major chord will contain the notes C, E and G. Major chords are said to have a happy sound.

Power Chords

Power chords contain only two distinct notes: the root note and the note at it's 5th. It is used extensively in metal and punk music but is also quite common in other genres. Since the 3rd interval is not played power chords are neither major nor minor. Power chords do not sound either happy or sad instead they tend to sound like one big note.

Suspended Chords

Suspended chords, commonly called sus chords, are formed by dropping the third or minor third from a chord and instead playing the second (called a sus2 chord) or the fourth (known as a sus4 chord). Since sus chords do not contain a third or minor third interval they have neither a major or minor tonality. In other words they do not sound happy or sad and instead tend to sound rather tense.

Moveable Chords

A moveable chord is one that does not utilize any open notes. By only playing fretted notes the chord can be moved up and down the fretboard. The shape of the chord stays the same but the pitch is changed.

Barre Chords

A barre chord is when a finger is used to press down more than one string. Barre chords are a type of moveable chord form. The two most common moveable chords are the E shape and the A shape, which are named after the open chords on which they are based.
