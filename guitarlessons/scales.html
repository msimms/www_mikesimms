Scales

Definition

A musical scale is a set of notes ordered by pitch and played in sequence. Knowledge of those scales which are common in western music is essential for anyone who wishes to play lead guitar as well as anyone interested in composition or improvisation.

Diatonic Scales

Diatonic scales are seven note scales comprising five whole steps and two half steps. They are the fundamental scales of western music.

Major Scale

The major scale is built by taking a root note and following this pattern of whole steps and half steps: w-w-h-w-w-w-h.

Example: A Major

Natural Minor Scale

The natural minor scale is built by taking a root note and following this pattern of whole steps and half steps: w-h-w-w-w-h-w.

Example: A Minor
e -----------------
b -----------------
g -----------------
d -------------5-7-
a -------5-7-8-----
e -5-7-8-----------

Harmonic Minor Scale

The harmonic minor is the same as the natural minor scale except that the 7th interval is raised a half-step.

Example: A Minor
e -----------------
b -----------------
g -----------------
d -------------6-7-
a -------5-7-8-----
e -5-7-8-----------

Scale Boxes

When playing the guitar it is useful to view scales as boxes which span four or five frets as well as all six strings. Many guitar instructors and most lesson books will attempt to assign names to these boxes. However as each box can indicate different scales or modes they are presented here with arbitrary names.

This example can be known as either A Major or F# Minor.

Pentatonic Scales

Pentatonic scales are five note scales.

Major Pentatonic

The Major Pentatonic scale is constructed by removing the 4th and 7th intervals from a standard major scale.

Minor Pentatonic

The Minor Pentatonic scale is constructed by removing the 2nd and 6th intervals from a natural minor scale.
